On LLMs

Pratik Deoghare

Nothing in life is to be feared, it is only to be understood. Now is the time to understand more, so that we may fear less. -- Marie Curie

I am neither pro nor anti-llms. Nobody knows how things will turn out. Only time will tell what happens. Instead of subscribing to a narrative and chosing sides I have chosen to have microstabs at the thing by making various observations.

Are llms here to stay? Yes. They have text capabilities that are hard or impossible to replicate any other way.

Are llms AI? No. “AI” is a marketing term. I would expect AI to be able to solve Sudoku. LLMs by themselves cannot do that. They can, however, use "function calling" to solve Sudoku. They cannot even call functions themselves. You have to prompt them to output in some format, another program parses the formatted output and calls functions.

Is there software now that wouldn’t exist without llms? Yes. I wanted an Emacs mode for a notation I use. I didn’t have time to learn enough Emacs to implement it myself. I asked ChatGPT, and it created a working implementation for me. It would have been hard to find anybody who would be interested in building it for free or as quickly. There must be a lot of programs like this out there.

We have lost valuable online communities. They might come back only after it is understood by sufficient number of people what we have lost. That might take a very long time. I am thinking it will be centuries before they come back. On stackoverflow I could ask questions. I could answer questions. Asking and answering got you reputation. I could see other people with broader and deeper knowledge answering questions. I saw Jon Skeet and others. I hadn't even thought you could be that good. In the world where everybody is using chatbots how will I know who is a great programmer? How great it is possible to be? Where do I stand? Who will I imitate?

Will reliance on llms to write code make us weak and stupid? Yes. How many of us can hunt? I rely on supermarket to feed me.

The Donkey in the Tiger-skin? Some people use llms to appear smarter than they are in discussions and to pretend to know things that they don't. This reminds me of story of The Donkey in the Tiger-skin.

Do I use llms? Code for my personal projects is written entirely by hand. This blog is entirely written by hand. Except one time I fed the blog to claude to change stylesheet. It made other changes that I didn't like. Some of those might have escaped my notice while reverting the older blog posts. If you see an emdash or a semicolon it is not from me.
